## üìñ Overview

In just eight weeks between January and February 2026, ten major open-weight LLM architectures were released.<br>
GLM-5 recorded benchmark scores on par with GPT-5.2 and Claude Opus 4.6.<br>
Step 3.5 Flash surpassed DeepSeek V3.2 ‚Äî a model three times its size.

**The performance gap between proprietary models (AI models developed and served behind closed doors by companies, such as GPT and Claude)<br>
and open-weight models (AI models whose core trained "weights" are publicly available,<br>
allowing anyone to download and run them) has already vanished.**

The structural change triggered by this fact goes far beyond "more model choices."<br>
The axis of competition is shifting from **"which model is smartest" to "where inference is executed and who controls the data."**

This book unpacks this structural shift from four perspectives:

- **Evidence of performance convergence** ‚Äî Why the gap has vanished now
- **The new axes of competition** ‚Äî Efficiency, speed, on-device, privacy
- **Enterprise strategy implications** ‚Äî The new concept of an Inference Location Portfolio
- **Irreversible consumer behavior change** ‚Äî From subscription fatigue to the irreversible migration to on-device AI

In the final chapter, all of these are connected to the author's Depth & Velocity (D&V) methodology,<br>
charting a new horizon for business development in the Edge AI era.

## üîë Core of This Book

### Core Thesis

The benchmark performance gap between open-weight LLMs and proprietary models has effectively vanished.<br>
The question is no longer "which model is best" but<br>
**"where inference is executed and who controls the data."**<br>
The answer will determine the trajectory of enterprise AI strategy and consumer technology for the next decade.<br>

This book argues that economic, psychological, and technological structural forces<br>
make edge AI and on-device inference not merely **"possible" but "inevitable."**

### Inference Location Portfolio (Three-Tier Model)

| Tier | Deployment | Use Case | Example Models |
|------|------------|----------|----------------|
| Tier 1 | Cloud API | Highest-accuracy decisions, instant access to latest models, experimental workloads | GPT-5.2, Claude Opus 4.6 |
| Tier 2 | On-Premise / Private Cloud | Confidential data processing, regulatory compliance, predictable high-volume workloads | GLM-5 (744B MoE), Qwen3.5 class |
| Tier 3 | Edge / On-Device | Real-time operations, offline environments, factories, retail, vehicles | Nanbeige 4.1 3B class, quantized 7B‚Äì14B |

### Consumer Flywheel

Subscription fatigue (economic motive)
‚Üí User tries on-device AI
‚Üí Experiences the comfort of privacy
‚Üí Gets accustomed to instant response
‚Üí Discovers it works offline
‚Üí A sense of "my own AI" takes root
‚Üí Cancels cloud AI subscription
‚Üí Commitment to on-device deepens further

**Once this flywheel begins to turn, a structural return to cloud AI becomes extremely unlikely ‚Äî<br>
because all five forces act in one direction only.**
